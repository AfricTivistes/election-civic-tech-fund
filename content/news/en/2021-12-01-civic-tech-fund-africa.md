# Civic Tech Fund Africa: New Grant Mechanism for Innovative Citizen Initiatives

The Charter Project Africa (CPA) launched the **Civic Tech Fund Africa** on Tuesday, November 30, 2021. This is a new funding mechanism for innovative citizen initiatives, making civic tech a facilitation tool for inclusive public decision-making and strengthening democratic governance in Africa.

## Citizen participation, a driver of change

Citizen participation is a powerful lever for driving social change and strengthening democratic governance. In Africa, the development of civic technologies and youth digital engagement are tools that can promote citizen participation.

Thanks to the Civic Tech Fund Africa, African civil society now has a new mode of funding for civic initiatives to support democratic governance. It is a fund designed to facilitate access for pioneers to a range of opportunities:
- A **seed grant**
- **Technological support**
- **Data assistance**
- Contribution from **policy experts**

## Implementing Civic Tech solutions

This fund is launched by the **Charter Project Africa**, an African initiative with two objectives:
1. Making citizens' voices heard through civic technologies
2. Encouraging participation in the implementation of the African Charter on Democracy, Elections and Governance (ACDEG)

The ACDEG is itself a tool of good governance through the promotion of citizen participation, adopted by African States.

## Supported by the European Union

The fund is supported by the European Union and is initially accessible to eleven African Union member countries:
- South Africa
- Benin
- Botswana
- Cabo Verde
- Ethiopia
- Kenya
- Nigeria
- Senegal
- Sudan
- Tanzania
- Zambia

## Encouraging youth innovation

Alban Biaussat, from the European Union Delegation to the African Union, highlights the program's ambition:

> *« The Civic Tech Fund Africa aims to encourage and motivate young citizens of African countries to innovate and implement civic tech solutions to engage with AU institutions. »*

## Strengthening existing projects

The fund is not only intended to award grants for the creation of outstanding initiatives, but to **strengthen existing projects** across the continent.

Salah S. Hammad, Head of the Secretariat of the African Governance Architecture (AGA), emphasizes the importance of civil society:

> *« Without the engagement of civil society and citizen participation, we cannot achieve the goals we have set ourselves in terms of democratic governance. »*

## A strategic initiative for Africa

The Civic Tech Fund Africa represents an important step in implementing the African Charter on Democracy, Elections and Governance. It aims to:
- Strengthen democratic governance
- Promote citizen participation
- Encourage technological innovation
- Support African youth
