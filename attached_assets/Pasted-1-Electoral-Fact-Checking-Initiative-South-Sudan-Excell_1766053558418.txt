Over 9 months, the project co-develops and deploys a multilingual, open-source digital platform offering interactive civic education tools, electoral information, and incident reporting for young people.
It will train 100 young people—including at least 60% young women—as Digital Civic Ambassadors across Puntland, Galmudug, North-East, and Mogadishu in Somalia, who will lead awareness campaigns reaching over 20,000 young people nationwide.
The initiative fosters better civic knowledge, digital participation, electoral observation, and public trust in democratic processes in Somalia.
Target groups: Somali youth, young women, and young adults
 Project duration: 9 months
Expected results:
1 DoorashoKaab platform co-developed with youth and launched for web and mobile
≥15,000 registered and active users
≥70% of users reporting better access to reliable electoral information
100 Digital Civic Ambassadors trained (≥60% women)
≥90% ambassadors demonstrating improved civic knowledge
10 regional ambassador teams ready to conduct civic campaigns
20,000 youths reached (≥50% women)
≥50% of participants demonstrating improved understanding of rights and responsibilities
1 learning report with lessons documented and shared with donors and partners

11) Inclusive Voices – Hopeline Foundation (🇸🇴 Somalie) - ENG✅

The project aims to strengthen electoral awareness, participation, and leadership among Somali youth and women in the districts of Hodan, Waberi, and Kahda in Mogadishu over eight months.
These districts were selected due to their high concentration of young people and internally displaced persons (IDPs), limited access to civic infrastructure, and presence of active local development partners.
Using digital storytelling, mobile civic education, community dialogue forums, leadership training, and civic education campaigns, the project improves knowledge, confidence, and empowerment; promotes equitable political participation; amplifies the voices of women and youth; and helps prevent hateful or discriminatory speech during Somalia’s upcoming electoral processes.
Target groups: youth, first-time voters, women in urban/peri-urban marginalised areas, community leaders, influencers, elders, electoral bodies, CSOs, general public
 Project duration: 8 months
Expected results:
Over 1,000 citizens reached through civic education and tailored SMS messages
300 women actively engaged in electoral discussions
15 civic champions trained and supported
5 digital stories viewed by over 25,000 online users
